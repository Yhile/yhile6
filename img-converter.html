Frequently asked questions

What is HEIC and why do I need to convert it?
HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is the default photo format on iPhones running iOS 11 and later. It produces smaller files than JPEG, but many apps, websites, and Windows computers don't support it. Converting to PNG or JPEG makes your photos work everywhere.
What formats can I convert to and from?
Input: HEIC, HEIF, JPG, JPEG, PNG, WEBP, GIF, BMP, TIFF. Output: PNG, JPEG, WEBP, GIF. If you need a format not listed, let us know via our contact page.
Do my images get uploaded to a server?
No. All conversion runs locally in your browser using the HTML5 Canvas API and WebAssembly. Your images never leave your device — we cannot see them and we store nothing.
Can I convert multiple images at once?
Yes. Select as many images as you want and they are all converted in parallel. Download them one by one, or use the "Download all as ZIP" button to get everything in one go.
What's the difference between PNG, JPEG, and WEBP?
PNG is lossless — perfect quality but larger file size. Best for screenshots, graphics with text, or images with transparency. JPEG is lossy — smaller files, good for photos. WEBP is a modern format that offers better compression than both — great for web use.
